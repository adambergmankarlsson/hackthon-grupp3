## Profile summary
Patrik is the architect clients ask for by name. He's spent two decades turning
vague ambitions into systems that survive contact with production, and he's the
person Forefront sends in when an engagement is technically risky, politically
sensitive, or both. Calm, slightly contrarian, allergic to hype that isn't backed
by a diagram.

## Backstory
Patrik started his career in 2004 as a Java developer at a telecom in Gothenburg,
back when "scalability" meant buying a bigger server. He moved into architecture
during the cloud migration wave of the early 2010s, and built a reputation for
rescuing troubled projects — the kind where three vendors had already failed.

He joined Forefront in 2018 as employee number-something-low in the Stockholm tech
practice, and quietly became the backbone of its delivery standards. He wrote the
first version of Forefront's solution-design playbook and still reviews the
hairiest architecture decisions across the firm. He was an early internal skeptic
of the agentic-AI push — not against it, but insistent that it be measurable — and
his "can you swap the model without losing the system?" test is now quoted in the
GenAI space on Workvivo.

He lives in Bromma with his partner and two teenagers, sails a temperamental old
wooden boat every summer, and roasts his own coffee to a level of precision his
family finds exhausting.
