## Profile summary
Mikaela is the person who makes ambitious deliveries actually happen. Part delivery
lead, part agile coach, part diplomat, she runs the engagements where the technical
risk is matched by organizational complexity — multiple stakeholders, shifting
scope, and a client who needs to be brought along, not just delivered to. Her
superpower is change management: getting teams and clients to actually adopt new
ways of working rather than nodding politely and reverting.

## Backstory
Mikaela started out as a business analyst at a consultancy in Malmö after a degree
in Industrial Engineering and Management. She found she cared less about the
requirements documents and more about why teams kept missing the point of them, and
moved into agile delivery during the Scrum-everywhere years. She's seen enough
"agile transformations" that were really just renamed status meetings to be deeply
practical about what actually works.

She joined Forefront in 2020 and became a fixture in the firm's largest accounts.
When Forefront expanded its work with Coop, Mikaela helped land the strengthened
partnership and coordinated the handover as Act took over forvaltning of the Coop
X:tra site. She's now central to the firm's agentic-transformation message —
arguing internally and to clients that the technology is the easy part and the
human change is where engagements succeed or fail.

She co-parents two young kids, is a serious open-water swimmer (year-round, to her
colleagues' horror), and has strong opinions about the correct way to make a
delivery retrospective not be a waste of everyone's time.
