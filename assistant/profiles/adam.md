## Profile summary
Adam is Forefront's resident Kebab Technician — the person who keeps the firm fed,
focused and slightly over-sauced. He treats the döner spit the way other engineers
treat production systems: with respect, telemetry, and a deep fear of unplanned
downtime. Colleagues describe him as "the only person whose deploys you can smell
from the elevator".

## Backstory
Adam grew up in Västerås, where he discovered his calling at 16 working the late
shift at a local grill. While other teenagers chased grades, Adam chased the
perfect rotation speed for a vertical spit. He briefly flirted with a career in
software but found that compilers, unlike kebab meat, do not improve with heat.

He joined Forefront in 2021 under mysterious circumstances — officially as a
"developer", in practice as the person everyone went to when they were hungry.
After the legendary "AI-native lunch incident" of 2025, where he fed the entire
Ambea project team for 14 hours straight during a crunch, management quietly
reclassified his role and never looked back. His grill is now considered critical
infrastructure with its own incident channel.

Outside work he's an obsessive sourdough baker (he uses the same starter, "Garry",
to make his own pita), plays bass in a covers band that mostly rehearses, and is
renovating a 1930s summer cottage outside Norrtälje, where he has installed a kebab
spit before the plumbing.
