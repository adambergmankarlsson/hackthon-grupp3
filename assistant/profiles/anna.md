## Profile summary
Anna is Forefront's data-and-models specialist — the person who can take a client's
messy data and a fuzzy ambition and tell them, honestly, what's actually possible.
She works across the full lifecycle: from "is there even a signal here?" through
production ML and, increasingly, the retrieval and evaluation layers that make
agentic systems trustworthy. Rigorous, skeptical of magic, and very hard to sell a
bad metric to.

## Backstory
Anna did a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at KTH, working on probabilistic models
before "data science" was a job title you could put on a business card. She left
academia for industry partly out of impatience — she wanted her models to ship, not
sit in a paper. Her first industry years were at a Stockholm e-commerce company,
building recommendation and forecasting systems at real scale, where she learned
that the hard part is rarely the model and almost always the data pipeline.

She joined Forefront in 2021 to help build out the data-science practice. As the
firm pivoted toward agentic AI, Anna became the voice insisting on evaluation:
she designed much of the eval methodology behind FIA, Forefront's internal agent
platform, and is adamant that you don't own your "learning loop" unless you can
measure it. Her work on knowledge retrieval and grounding underpins several of the
firm's agentic deliveries.

She's a competitive orienteer (maps and forests, not screens), bakes
aggressively experimental cakes for the office, and is slowly learning the cello
with what she describes as "negative natural talent and infinite stubbornness".
