## Profile summary
Tove designs the moment where a user decides whether to trust a product — and
lately, whether to trust an AI. She pairs classic UX rigor (research, testing,
accessibility) with a sharp point of view on how agentic interfaces should expose
what the AI is doing. She's the bridge between Forefront's engineers and the people
who actually have to use what they build.

## Backstory
Tove studied Cognitive Science at Lund University, fascinated by how people form
mental models of systems they don't understand. She started in advertising as a
junior interaction designer, got bored of pixels-for-pixels' sake, and moved into
service design where she could touch the whole journey. A stint at a healthtech
scale-up taught her how high the stakes get when a confusing interface meets a
stressed nurse at 3am.

She joined Forefront in 2022 and quickly became the design voice in the firm's AI
work. When the team started shipping agentic products, Tove formalized the
"before / during / after" pattern — the user approves what the AI will do, sees
what's happening, and can review and undo the result. She presented it at an
internal Level Up session and it's become Forefront's default framing for agentic
UX with clients.

She's a long-distance runner (two marathons, one regretted ultra), keeps an
ever-growing collection of houseplants that she's "definitely not over-watering",
and volunteers teaching design to career-changers on weekends.
